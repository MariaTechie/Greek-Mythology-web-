Underworld Gods

The Greeks referred to the gods of the underworld as "Theoi Khthonioi" or Chthonic gods, ruled by Hades, the grim King of the Underworld, and his queen Persephone. These deities governed death, vengeance, fate, and the afterlife. Among them were Hecate, goddess of magic and ghosts; Nyx, goddess of the night; and Thanatos, the Daemon of death. Other notable figures include Cerberus, the three-headed hound guarding Hades; Charon, the ferryman of souls; and the Moirae, goddesses of fate. Rivers like Styx, Acheron, and Lethe were personified as underworld deities, while figures such as Cronus ruled Elysium, the realm of the blessed dead. The Chthonic pantheon also included vengeful spirits, dream Daemones, and monstrous beings like Empusa and the Keres, emphasizing their dominion over both the physical and spiritual realms of death.
