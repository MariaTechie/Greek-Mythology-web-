Hera

Hera, the Olympian queen of the gods, was the goddess of marriage, women, the sky, and the stars of heaven. She is often depicted as a regal woman with a crown, a lotus-tipped scepter, and sometimes accompanied by animals like lions, cuckoos, or hawks. Hera's myths include her marriage to Zeus, who seduced her as a cuckoo, the birth of Hephaestus, her persecution of Zeus's lovers and their children (like Heracles and Dionysus), and the punishment of Ixion. She assisted the Argonauts, participated in the Judgment of Paris, and supported the Greeks in the Trojan War.
